Optional Major Advice

Choosing the right major can significantly impact your career in electricity, gas, water, and waste services. Here are some recommended majors and fields of study:

Electrical Engineering: Pursue a degree in Electrical Engineering to specialize in the generation, distribution, and management of electrical power.
Environmental Engineering: Environmental Engineering focuses on sustainable water treatment and waste management practices, making it relevant to utility services.
Civil Engineering: Civil Engineering provides expertise in infrastructure development and maintenance, including water supply and waste management systems.
Mechanical Engineering: Mechanical Engineering majors can specialize in utility equipment maintenance and repair.
Energy Management: This major focuses on optimizing energy efficiency and renewable energy integration, which is relevant to the electricity and gas sectors.
Environmental Science: Consider a major in Environmental Science for a broader understanding of environmental regulations and sustainability in utility services.
Project Management: Pursue a major in Project Management for roles involving project planning and execution in utility infrastructure development.
Business Administration: A degree in Business Administration with a focus on operations or utility management can be valuable for leadership roles in the utility sector.
Public Relations and Communications: If you aspire to work in public relations and customer engagement within utility services, a major in Public Relations or Communications is beneficial.
Data Science or Data Analytics: In an era of data-driven decision-making, majors related to data science or data analytics can be relevant for roles involving data analysis and resource optimization.
Occupational Health and Safety: For roles involving safety management and compliance in utility environments, a major in Occupational Health and Safety is advantageous.
